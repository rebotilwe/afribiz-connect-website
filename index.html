Frequently Asked Questions

Q. How do I enter the South African market?
A.

Entering the South African market requires careful planning and local expertise. Our market entry process includes:

  • Market feasibility study
  • Regulatory compliance analysis
  • Partner identification
  • Entry strategy development
  • Local network activation
  • Business registration support
  • Ongoing advisory services
  • Growth strategy planning
Q. What African countries do you cover?
A.

We have extensive experience and local networks across multiple African countries, including:

  • South Africa
  • Kenya
  • Nigeria
  • Ghana
  • Botswana
  • Zambia
  • Mozambique
  • Namibia

We can also assist with market entry into other African countries through our extensive partner network.

Q. How long does it take to enter a new African market?
A.

The timeline varies depending on the country, industry, and complexity of your business. Generally, the process includes:

  • Market research: 2-4 weeks
  • Strategy development: 2-3 weeks
  • Partner identification: 3-6 weeks
  • Regulatory approvals: 4-12 weeks
  • Business setup: 2-6 weeks

We can provide a more accurate timeline after our initial consultation.

Q. What are the main challenges of doing business in Africa?
A.

While Africa offers tremendous opportunities, businesses often face challenges such as:

  • Complex regulatory environments
  • Cultural and language barriers
  • Finding reliable local partners
  • Supply chain complexities
  • Currency volatility
  • Infrastructure limitations
  • Market intelligence gaps

Our expertise helps you navigate these challenges effectively.

Q. How much does market entry consulting cost?
A.

Our consulting fees vary based on the scope of work, target countries, and complexity of your project. We offer flexible engagement models:

  • Market research reports: Fixed fee
  • Strategy consulting: Project-based
  • Implementation support: Retainer
  • Ongoing advisory: Monthly retainer

Contact us for a customized quote based on your specific needs.
